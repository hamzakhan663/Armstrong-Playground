What even are Armstrong Numbers?

{% comment %} Descriptive messages about Armstrong Numbers {% endcomment %}

Armstrong numbers, also known as narcissistic numbers or plenary numbers, are a special type of number in mathematics. These numbers are named after Michael F. Armstrong, who first introduced them in a letter to Dorrie in 1969. An Armstrong number is a number that is equal to the sum of its own digits each raised to the power of the number of digits in the number itself. For example, 153 is an Armstrong number because 1^3 + 5^3 + 3^3 = 153.

Armstrong numbers are relatively rare and exist only for certain combinations of digits. The number of Armstrong numbers is finite. Armstrong numbers are self-descriptive in the sense that they exhibit a unique property related to their digits. Armstrong numbers are used in recreational mathematics and programming exercises as a challenge to find them efficiently.

Armstrong numbers are fascinating mathematical entities that demonstrate interesting properties related to the digits comprising them. Despite being relatively rare, they are widely studied and used in various mathematical contexts, making them an intriguing topic for exploration.
